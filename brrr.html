About Lions

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large felid of the genus Panthera.
It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail It is sexually dimorphic;
adult male lions have a prominent mane. With a typical head-to-body length of 184 to 208 cm (72 to 82 in) they are larger than females at 160 to 184 cm (63 to 72 in)

Etymology

The word 'lion' is derived from Latin: leo and Ancient Greek: λέων (leon).The word lavi (Hebrew: לָבִיא‎) may also be related. The generic name Panthera is traceable to the classical Latin word 'panthēra' and the ancient Greek word πάνθηρ 'panther'.
